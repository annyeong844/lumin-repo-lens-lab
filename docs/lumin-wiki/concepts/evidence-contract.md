# Evidence Contract

Lumin's core rule is evidence before claims. A result should say what was
observed, what was not observed, and what could not be checked.

## Rules

- Resolved evidence may support graph edges.
- Candidate evidence may guide review but must not become proof.
- Unsupported evidence must name the family and reason.
- Missing evidence must be labeled as missing, not interpreted as absence.
- Summary artifacts should point to raw evidence when a claim needs audit.

## Test Implication

A strong test checks both sides:

- the intended evidence appears
- the forbidden stronger claim does not appear

Examples include "unsupported import is recorded" plus "no concrete graph edge
is created," or "suppressed candidate is recorded" plus "no cue card is
promoted."
